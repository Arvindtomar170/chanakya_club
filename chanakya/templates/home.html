Importance of policy analysis and research

Policy at any level is the set of government directives intended to shape the decisions and actions of individuals, organizations, and government agencies. As such, the policy can be established by legislation, executive orders, judicial rulings, guidelines and regulations, rulemaking, agency memos, signing statements, agency circulars, and other types of official statements. Different agencies often create incompatible, redundant, or conflicting information policies.

In a time of tight government budgets, donors and clients want to know whether the research they fund makes any difference to public policy choices and, ultimately, to people’s lives. Yet the methodologies for evaluating the impact of social science research on policy choices and policy outcomes are not well-developed. The complexity of the policy process and the general nature of much policy research makes it difficult to attribute policy decisions or policy outcomes to specific research findings.

'Research' is defined in many places, but a very general description involves work carried out to answer specific issues and questions, or widen understanding, or add to the body of knowledge.

Information policy research is a critical tool in the arsenal of library and information science researchers. As developments occur in information access, use, technology, and management, information policies require more attention and research.

Policy research can have a significant impact on policymaking, just not necessarily on discrete choices nor in the linear sequence that researchers and donors would like to see. Generally, Policymakers frequently use research less to dictate specific solutions than to help them think about issues and define the scope of problems and possible responses. Thus, research information provides a diffuse ‘enlightenment’ function, providing an understanding and interpretation of the data and the situation that is critical to the policy decision.

Policy analysis plays an important role in helping to define and outline the goals of a proposed policy and in identifying similarities and differences in expected outcomes and estimated costs with competing for alternative policies. Many public policies are designed to solve both current and future problems, and thus policy analysis attempts to forecast future needs based on past and present conditions. Policy outcomes can be found in a variety of different forms—tangible outputs and less-tangible outputs for which the impacts are more difficult to measure. In many cases, it is difficult to determine if the policy itself resulted in desired change or if other exogenous or external factors were the most direct cause. Nevertheless, it is important to determine if a policy is responsible for the desired change; otherwise, there would be no need for the policy.

The differences between textbook-based policy research and how policy research might be implemented should be discussed, as is the inter-connectedness of information policies and ways to describe impacts of information policies. Researchers and professionals should be giving attention to using policy research on a day-to-day basis.

Vision

Our central vision is to increase the awareness of policy research and the impacts of policy in the IITI community specifically and in the country in general. Further, our vision is to provide research internships, ground-level surveys, and field analysis for developing the required skills to the interested students. They should understand the practical component of the application part of their attained knowledge as much as they are aware theoretically.

The club will be presenting problem statements to work upon and providing the ethical solutions with connective brainstorming with the help of human resources of students from interdisciplinary fields. The objectives of CPPC are to develop substantive policy options on matters relevant to the Indian polity, economy, and society; to provide advisory services to governments, public bodies, and other institutions; and to disseminate information on policy issues through various channels. The governing board of CPPC expects formal guidance from various public figures from the Indian government, academia, and industry to carry on this club in IIT Indore.

6. Committees

The Club may at its Annual General Meeting elect Working Committees which shall operate under such terms as the Club may from time to time determine. The number of members of any Committee and its composition shall be determined by the Club but shall include all elected Officers of the Club. The Club may also appoint Sub-Committees as it may deem necessary. The Club shall also, at its Annual General Meeting, elect a Disciplinary Board which shall be comprised of three persons, and which shall deal with any disciplinary matters which arise in accordance.
